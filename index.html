Quick Hummus recipe

What is Hummus?

Hummus is a popular Middle Eastern dip or spread made primarily from mashed cooked chickpeas blended with tahini ( a paste of ground sesame seeds), lemon juice, and garlic. It is known for its smooth, creamy texture and is often garnished with olive oil, a sprinkle of paprika, and whole chickpeas.

This recipe makes quick, tasty hummus, with no messing. It has been adapted from a number of different recipes that I have read over the years. Hummus is a delicious thick paste used heavily in Greek and Middle Eastern dishes. It is very tasty with salad, grilled meats and pitta breads.

Ingredients

  • 1 can ( 400g ) of chick peas ( Garbanzo beans )
  • 175g of tahini
  • 6 sundried tomatoes
  • Half a red pepper
  • A pinch of Cayenne pepper
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • A dash of olive oil

Instructions

  1. Remove the skin from the garlic, and chop coarsely.
  2. Remove all the seeds and stalk from the pepper, and chop coarsely.
  3. Add all the ingredients into a food processor.
  4. Process all the ingredients into a paste.
  5. If you want a coarse " chunky " Hummus, process it for a short time.
  6. If you want a smooth Hummus, process it for a longer time.
  7. For a different flavour, you could try blending in a small measure of lemon and coriander, chili pepper, lime and chipotle, harissa and mint, or spinach and feta cheese. Experiment and see what works for you.
  8. For storage put in the refrigerate the finished hummus in a sealed container. You should be able to use it for about a week after you've made it. If it starts to become fizzy, you should definitely discard it. Hummus is suitable for freezing; you should thaw it and use it within a couple of months.

Nutrition Facts

Hummus is nutritious, providing a good source of protein, fiber, and healthy fats, along with essential vitamins and minerals like iron, manganese, copper, and folate. A typical 2-tablespoon serving contains about 70 calories, 6 grams of fat, 5 grams of carbohydrates, and 2 grams of protein. Its nutritional value can vary depending on the specific brand or recipe, but it is often a healthier alternative to other spreads and dips.
